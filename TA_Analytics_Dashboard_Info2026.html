Chapter 2 · Diagnostic Analytics

Why is the pipeline underperforming?

Five root causes. Correlated evidence. No speculation — every diagnosis is traceable back to the data.

2.1Root Cause Analysis — The Five Failure Modes
Diagnostic Summary
The data reveals five compounding failure modes: a collapsed L1→L2 funnel (76% drop), an over-reliance on low-quality agency sourcing, a 22.6% position drop rate due to poor requisition governance, a 36.8% offer-to-join fall-off, and severe geographic concentration risk in Chennai. These are not independent — they compound each other.
Root Cause #1 — The L1 Funnel Collapse
⚠ Problem: 76% of L1 candidates never reach L2 (630 of 829 lost)
Cause A — Sourcing Misalignment
Agency-sourced profiles (44% of pipeline) are submitted without JD fitment checks. Agencies optimise for volume, not match quality. Evidence: agency hire rate = 0.7% vs referral = 9.1%
Cause B — No Standardised L1 Scorecard
Without calibrated evaluation rubrics, L1 decisions are subjective. This produces inconsistent pass/fail thresholds across panels and positions, inflating rejection counts.
Contributing Factor
172 rescheduled events suggest panel availability bottlenecks that degrade candidate experience and increase ghosting rates
Cause C — JD-Candidate Gap
Position skills list vs. candidate skills shows mismatch patterns in ETL, Azure, and niche tech stacks. Active sourcing is pulling generic profiles for specialised roles.
Impact Metric
76%
L1 to L2 Drop Rate
630 candidates lost between L1 and L2. At typical industry rate of 40–50%, you should be advancing ~415 to L2. The 215-candidate gap represents direct hiring capacity loss.
Correlated Signal

Total Rejections in Pipeline
328 formal rejections logged — predominantly at Recruiter Screening (leading to L1) and L1 stage, confirming early funnel is the primary value destruction zone.

Decision Framework
Actions are ranked by projected hire uplift per unit of effort. The methodology: each action's predicted hire increase is estimated using current conversion rate data, industry benchmarks, and pipeline volumes. Actions with high impact + low effort are prioritised regardless of complexity perception.

Launch a Structured Employee Referral Program

Create role-specific evaluation rubrics with 5-point scales for technical, communication, and fitment dimensions. Conduct a panel calibration workshop (half-day). Monitor weekly L1→L2 rate as primary KPI. Target: move from 24% to 40% conversion.

Introduce a 3-touch engagement model: Day 1 (offer debrief call), Day 7 (counter-offer risk check), Day 14 (onboarding readiness). Assign each offer to a named recruiter as single point of contact. Track revocation reasons in ATS.
